What Are Concussions?

A concussion is a type of mild traumatic brain injury (TBI) that occurs when the brain experiences a sudden, rapid movement within the skull, often due to a blow, jolt, or impact to the head or body. This abrupt movement can disrupt normal brain function temporarily. Concussions are common, particularly in activities such as sports, falls, or vehicle accidents.

Symptoms of Concussion

  • Altered Mental State: Individuals with a concussion may experience temporary confusion, disorientation, or a feeling of being dazed. They might have trouble concentrating, memory problems, or difficulty processing information.
  • Physical Symptoms: Common physical symptoms include headache, dizziness, nausea, vomiting, blurred vision, sensitivity to light or noise, and balance problems. Some individuals may also report a "pressure" sensation in the head.
  • Emotional and Behavioral Changes: Concussions can lead to emotional disturbances such as irritability, mood swings, anxiety, depression, and changes in sleep patterns (insomnia or excessive sleepiness).
  • Loss of Consciousness: Although loss of consciousness is not always present in concussions, it can occur in some cases, even if only for a brief period.
  • Amnesia: Temporary memory loss, particularly regarding the events immediately before or after the injury (retrograde and anterograde amnesia, respectively), is a common feature of concussions.
